JOYFUL

A novel by Ingrid Fetell Lee

The Surprising Power of Ordinary Things to Create Extraordinary Happiness

We are often made to feel that the physical world has little or no impact on our inner joy. But what if the natural vibrancy of our surroundings is actually our most renewable and easily accessible source of joy?

While technology has moved our health and well-being immeasurably forward, it also robbed us of a wisdom that existed when our relationship to our environment wasn’t mediated by so many dials and controls. This we have built environments that lack elements that are essential to our well-being.

This is not about seeking joy in the far-flung corners of the world. This is an invitation to find more joy right where you are.

ENERGY

It’s not a surprise that we equate a feeling of energy with one of liveliness, vitality, and joy. Energy animates matter. It is the currency of life, transforming inert material into breathing, beating organisms. Simply to be alive is to vibrate with an essential dynamism. The more energy we have, the more we are able to play, create, love, lead, explore, rejoice, and engage with the world around us.

The liveliness of color helps us marshal the energy we need to learn, be productive, and grow. Bright color acts as a stimulant, a shot of caffeine for the eyes.

ABUNDANCE

“Like a kid in a candy store” is one of the most iconic images of joy in our culture, expressing the wild, almost delirious pleasure we take in being let loose in a bountiful world. When we are in moments of joy, our delight is so abundant.

The kind of abundance that really matters is not material accumulation but sensorial richness.The abundance aesthetic is defined by a layering of color, texture, and pattern, and you don’t need a lot of stuff to achieve it. Choosing abundance is not a moral failing. It’s an expression of deep, human delight. It’s an acknowledgment that we are here to d.o more than eke out an existence between birth and death. We are to live out Mae West’s famous aphorism, that “too much of a good thing can be wonderful.”

HARMONY

There is a deep simple joy found in harmony and order. Simply arranging similar objects in geometric configurations was enough to transform anything into a source of light. This might appear contradictory to energy, abundance and freedom, but joy is wild and free, but sometimes it’s also very organized.

Harmony offers visible evidence that someone cares enough about a place to invest in it. Harmony grounds and balances these more exuberant aesthetics in ways that can make them more potent. By noticing more hidden harmonies in the world, we may find a more understated kind of joy in our lives.

PLAY

Play is one of our greatest means of accessing delight and play etches itself deeply into our memories for good reason: it is the only known activity that humans engage in solely because it produces joy. However, as we age, we’re told to “quit playing around,” we have more homework than recess, and we learn that seriousness is prized over joy. While the topic of work-life balance comes up frequently in the media, play is largely absent from the conversation.

Aesthetically, the story of childhood is the story of the circle and the sphere. Round forms are magnetic and the most approachable shapes, with no sharp edges to risk injury, and curved shapes also have more affordances and ways to be used. Round objects offer a unique potential for discovery and delight and especially contrasts with the rigid angular grid shape of our built environment.

SURPRISE

Joy has a way of showing up when we least expect it. As we flow through the stream of daily life, tiny moments can capture our attention and turn our thoughts in a joyful direction. Surprise has a vital purpose: to quickly redirect our attention. Joyful surprises bring our attention away from ourselves and back out into the world, prompting us to approach and engage. They incite curiosity, spur exploration, and increase the chances we’ll interact with others in ways that keep the positive vibes flowing.

Surprise also intensifies our other emotions. It acts like a magnifying glass for joy, imbuing tiny pleasures with heightened significance. Harmony and surprise pair well together because they create a tension, a contrast between an object and its context. By snapping us out of our habitual thought patterns, a small surprise can rest our joy meters and allow us to see with new eyes.

TRANSCENDENCE

The physical experience of joy and well-being has an upward quality, upwardness has become a metaphor for positivity in our lives. The most significant force in our world is gravity and every movement we make is constrained by gravity’s irresistible pull. Though we roam freely in the horizontal plane, without technology, we’re unable to use above the ground.

Getting up above the ground changed our relationship to the earth and to the lives we live on it. Worries and petty disagreements seem to shrink. Along with objects that are elevated, lightness and airiness are metaphors for joy. Transcendence detaches us from the world, lofting us up above our routines. Yet, paradoxically, instead of distancing us from what we care about, it seems to bring us closer. Closer to others, closer to what feels truly important, closer, even, to ourselves.

MAGIC

Magic — and the permission to believe in it — is one of the true joys of childhood. But by the time we are adults, we are expected to keep both feet firmly planted in the rational world and to leave magic behind entirely. Magic can be valuable because it imbues life with a deeper sense of meaning. It weaves a story, where there’s an ultimate purpose.

Magical thinking can fuel our optimism about the future. It ruptures the membrane between possible and impossible, igniting our curiosity about the world we live in. The universe is full of magical things patiently waiting for our wits to grow sharper. Wonders never cease, as long as we are willing to look for them.

CELEBRATION

Celebrations mark the pinnacles of joy in our lives. At these moments, our joy seems to overflow, and we have an irresistible urge to bring others together to join in our delight. A celebration cultivates an atmosphere of inclusive delight. It’s a state of belonging, where for a few moments, every individual is united in the same effervescent joy.

At the heart of celebration is a kind of mathematical paradox: the more joy we share, the more it grows. The implication of this is that we should manage joy in the exact opposite way that we manage money. We should spend it all, at every chance we get. Because the more generous we are with our joy, the more we have for ourselves.

RENEWAL

We dream of a durable kind of happiness, a constant state of bliss. And while there are many things we can do to create a reservoir of joy that helps us amplify the highs and buffer the lows of everyday life, sometimes we have to accept that joy moves through our lives in an unpredictable way.

The lesson of renewal is that from small seeds, big things can grow. Out of ends, renewal creates beginnings. Out of destruction, creation. The drive towards joy is the drive toward life. Joy is our inner guide to the things that animate, stimulate, and sustain us. Simply put, joy is what makes life worth living.
